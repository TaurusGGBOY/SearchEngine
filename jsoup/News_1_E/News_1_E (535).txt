UNITED NATIONS April Xinhua Humanitarian aid workers in areas ravaged by Cyclone Idai are fighting to stave off the spread of cholera establishing treatment centers in Mozambique alone a UN spokesman said on Monday Of those sites nine are operational including in the hard hit port city of Beira said Stephane Dujarric spokesman for UN chief Antonio Guterres A cholera vaccination campaign will begin on this Wednesday Dujarric said Some cases have been reported in the last hours There is also a high risk of the spread of vector borne diseases with malaria cases also reported in the affected areas Cholera spread by contaminated food and water causes diarrhea that can result in death if not treated The number of confirmed deaths in areas suffering from Idai parts of Malawi Mozambique and Zimbabwe has topped the mark with Mozambique itself reporting fatalities The president of Zimbabwe Emmerson Mnangagwa said shortly after the cyclone struck late on March that he feared dead from the storm So far about fatalities have been confirmed in Zimbabwe Officials have said the toll may never be known because many bodies have been buried without registration many in shallow graves and many of those corpses have washed out to sea as have many others from the flooding rains accompanying Idai The Economic and Social Council will convene a Special Meeting on the response to Cyclone Idai in Mozambique Malawi and Zimbabwe said the spokesman Deputy Secretary General Amina Mohammed will address the meeting as well as Emergency Relief Coordinator Under Secretary General Mark Lowcock Dujarric told reporters during a regular briefing adding that the meeting will be held on Tuesday The meeting will discuss how to support a well coordinated response focusing on immediate humanitarian needs as well as sustainable and risk informed medium and long term recovery and reconstruction in the aftermath of the storm Dujarric said
